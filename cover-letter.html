Candidate letter — AI Cloud Native Forward Deployed Senior Manager / Associate Director
July 16, 2026

Accenture's forward-deployed engineering expansion points to a clear client need: enterprises do not need another AI demonstration; they need engineers and advisors who can enter the workflow, make the hidden constraints legible and convert ambition into a production system that people can trust and operate.

That is the work I have repeatedly been drawn toward. At Vape-Jet, I am leading AI-first operating transformation across production, quality, support, engineering issue flow, ERP, telemetry and a field population of approximately 1,000 robots. Through DudeWorth, I design agentic workflow patterns that combine retrieval, human judgment, escalation, evaluation and governance. At Amazon, I built 24/7 operating mechanisms around customer promises, standard work and rapid escalation. At Compunetics, I led high-reliability engineering and manufacturing systems where evidence, process control and customer consequence were inseparable.

My value in this role would be to connect four conversations that too often separate: the client's business workflow, the agent's technical architecture, the governance and evidence required for action, and the operating system that sustains value after the first production release. I work directly in that translation—mapping decisions, designing mechanisms, testing states and creating the cadence that turns new technology into adopted work.

I created the independent candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/Accenture/ to make that reasoning inspectable. Its Agent Load Path shows how I would structure discovery and release around five explicit contracts: work, context, authority, evidence and run ownership. The site also includes a 90-day entry plan and a practical review artifact; it is a transparent demonstration of my approach, not a claim about undisclosed Accenture processes.

I am currently based in Pittsburgh and actively relocating to the Tampa Bay area. The St. Petersburg hybrid model and client travel expectations align with that plan. I would welcome a working conversation about how Accenture wants its forward-deployed teams to balance client-specific speed with production assurance and reusable leverage across the Advanced Technology Centers.

Carpe diem,
