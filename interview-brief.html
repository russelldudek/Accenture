Interview thesis brief
Thesis: An agent is not deployed when it answers. It is deployed when the workflow can trust the crossing.

Mandate

Turn ambiguous client demand into production agent workflows that are useful, governed, observable and reusable across Accenture's delivery network.

Company moment

Accenture's 2026 FDE programs with Microsoft, Google Cloud, ServiceNow and SAP formalize a shoulder-to-shoulder delivery model: industry-specific use cases, direct client embedding, rapid prototyping, production activation, measurable outcomes and scaling. Accenture's “intelligent superhighway” research argues that sustained AI value depends on governed data, explicit decision logic, codified workflows, cloud-native modular architecture, governance roles and decision rights.

Hiring-manager risk analysis — private reasoning translated into affirmative contribution

The posting emphasizes long-tenured cloud-native software engineering and recent production agentic frameworks. The evidence should not be inflated into unverified platform tenure. The candidacy is strongest when evaluated for the combination the role actually needs: direct agentic workflow design, engineering and operations translation, high-reliability control systems, client-facing facilitation, production ownership thinking and adoption mechanisms. A bounded architecture/code working session is the right proof surface.

Working-session proposal

Take one supplied use case and, in 60–90 minutes, produce: workflow boundary, agent/deterministic/human split, provider abstraction seams, authority matrix, evaluation harness outline, observability and cost units, release posture and first production slice.

Executive interview questions

  1. Where does Accenture draw the boundary between FDE and conventional delivery?
  2. Which production handoff most often stalls: platform ownership, data, evaluation, security, adoption or economics?
  3. What must be reusable across clients, and what must remain deliberately client-specific?
  4. How are FDE teams measured after the first workload is live?
  5. Who owns the hold decision when autonomy demand exceeds available evidence?
  6. How do ATCs harvest engagement learning without creating an asset-production tax on delivery teams?
